Yandere Aesthetic

Yandere (ヤンデレ), derived from the Japanese words "yanderu" (to be mentally ill) and "deredere" (lovey-dovey), describes a character archetype with an unhealthy love-related obsession that often results in violent outbreaks triggered by unrequited love or disappointment.

Analog Horror

Analog Horror (or Analogue Horror) is the older counterpart of Digital Horror and a subgenre of Found Footage, which incorporates old "analog" styled media such as TV Broadcasts, VHS Tapes, and EAS while including horror elements that evoke feelings of dread, fear, and uncanniness.

Learn More

Weirdcore

Weirdcore is a surrealistic aesthetic centered around amateur or low-quality photography and/or visual images that have been constructed or edited to convey feelings of confusion, disorientation, dread, alienation and nostalgia or anemoia.

Learn More

Morute Aesthetic

Morute, a name combining the words "morbid" and "cute," is an aesthetic created in the early 2010's that expresses the dichotomy between the hyper-femininity and softness of a childhood/girlhood in the past with the darker imagery of true crime and American Gothic elements.

Learn More

Creepy Cute

This is a generalization of any morbidly cute aesthetics like Morute, Gurokawa, Jirai Kei, etc.
